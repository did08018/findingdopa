Nerve Grafts

Nerve grafts are designed to minimize immune rejection in Parkinson’s disease treatment. These grafts aim to be “invisible” to the body’s immune system.

One approach uses autologous peripheral nerve grafts implanted into affected brain regions, leveraging compatibility and reducing immune response.

Another strategy involves co-transplanting regulatory T cells, which reduce inflammation and improve graft survival.

These advancements pave the way for durable PD treatments that integrate immune modulation.

MSCs

Mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) support tissue repair, reduce inflammation, and are being studied for Parkinson’s disease.

They secrete neuroprotective factors, shift immune cells to neuroprotective states, and may stimulate endogenous repair mechanisms.

MSCs can be delivered via various methods and have shown early promise in trials, though long-term efficacy is under investigation.
