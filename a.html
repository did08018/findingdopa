ἄσπις aspis

* The Shield & The Snake, I cannot tell them apart, yet I walk with both.

In Greek, aspis carries a layered double meaning — both the ancient word for a warrior's shield and, through later association, the image of a venomous snake. Mechanisms of defense and agents of harm can mirror each other so closely that distinction collapses, and one moves forward carrying both, unable to set either down.

Beneath this lies a quieter unease: systems built to heal are also shaped by delay, by hesitation, by the long spaces between promise and arrival. Those waiting — especially for conditions without clear cure — remain suspended between hope and urgency, unable to tell whether what surrounds them is protection in motion or harm unfolding at a different pace.
