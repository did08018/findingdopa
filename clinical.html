A fix for Parkinson's disease is on the way. Keep fighting!

Nerve Grafts

Scientists are exploring nerve grafts to replace damaged neurons in Parkinson’s patients. These grafts may help re-establish lost connections and restore motor function over time.

MSCs

Mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) have anti-inflammatory properties and may slow disease progression. They’re also being tested for their ability to support dopamine-producing cells.

iPSCs

Induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) can be turned into dopamine-producing neurons. This personalized approach could lead to safer, patient-specific therapies.

Clinical Trials

Multiple clinical trials are underway to evaluate stem cell treatments for Parkinson’s. Results so far are promising, with some showing motor improvement and safety.

The Future

Combining cell therapies, gene editing, and brain-targeted delivery may offer a cure in the coming years. Research is accelerating toward lasting solutions.
