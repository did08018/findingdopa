Nerve Grafts

Nerve grafts aim to reduce immune rejection in Parkinson’s treatment by being invisible to the immune system. Autologous nerve tissue is often used to improve compatibility.

Co-transplanting regulatory T cells has shown promise in suppressing the immune reaction to surgical trauma, improving graft survival.

These strategies are being tested in clinical trials to enhance the safety and longevity of Parkinson’s therapies.

MSCs

Mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) are adult stem cells with regenerative and immunomodulatory abilities.

In Parkinson’s research, MSCs help protect neurons by secreting factors like BDNF and GDNF, reducing inflammation, and possibly aiding brain repair.

They're being tested in trials using various delivery methods, including intravenous and intrathecal injection.
