Nerve Grafts

Nerve grafts are used to replace or repair damaged neural pathways in Parkinson’s. They may reconnect circuits or provide scaffolding for new growth.

×

MSCs

Mesenchymal Stem Cells (MSCs) offer potential for repairing damaged tissue, reducing inflammation, and supporting neurogenesis in Parkinson's patients.

×

Dopamine Pathways

These pathways are critical for movement and mood. Parkinson’s disrupts them, but new therapies aim to restore or bypass these circuits.

×

Glial Cells

Glial cells support neurons and help maintain brain health. They're being studied for their role in neurodegeneration and recovery.

×

Brain Plasticity

Neuroplasticity is the brain’s ability to adapt and rewire. Enhancing plasticity could help Parkinson’s patients regain lost function.
