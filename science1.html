Nerve Grafts

Nerve grafts are designed to minimize immune rejection in Parkinson’s disease (PD) treatment. These grafts aim to be “invisible” to the body’s immune system, enhancing their integration and effectiveness.

One approach utilizes autologous peripheral nerve grafts, where a patient’s own nerve tissue is transplanted into the brain regions affected by PD, such as the substantia nigra...

MSCs: Mesenchymal stem/stromal cells

MSCs are a type of adult stem cell found in bone marrow, fat, and other tissues. They help reduce inflammation and support tissue repair...

They’re being researched in Parkinson’s for their potential to support dopaminergic neurons, reduce neuroinflammation, and stimulate brain repair...
