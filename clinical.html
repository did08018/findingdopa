Nerve Grafts

Scientists are exploring the transplantation of peripheral nerve tissue to stimulate regeneration in the brain’s dopaminergic system. Early results are promising and offer a potential support framework for restoring lost function.

Mesenchymal Stem Cells (MSCs)

MSCs are multipotent stem cells capable of reducing inflammation and secreting protective factors. Researchers hope they can provide neuroprotective effects or even differentiate into neural cells in Parkinson’s patients.

Gene Therapy

Gene therapy aims to correct or replace faulty genes responsible for dopamine production. Several clinical trials are testing its ability to slow or reverse Parkinson’s progression.

Dopaminergic Cells

Scientists are working to develop dopaminergic neurons from stem cells that can be implanted into the brain to restore lost function. These lab-grown cells are showing great promise in early studies.

Future Tech

Innovations such as brain-computer interfaces, nanomedicine, and adaptive neurostimulation may transform how we treat Parkinson’s Disease in the coming decade.
