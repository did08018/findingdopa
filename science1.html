Nerve Grafts
Nerve grafts aim to be “invisible” to the immune system, using either autologous peripheral nerve tissue or immune-modulating strategies like co-transplanting regulatory T cells. These techniques are designed to minimize rejection and inflammation while enhancing the survival of transplanted cells.

×

MSCs (Mesenchymal Stem/Stromal Cells)
MSCs are adult stem cells capable of reducing inflammation, supporting repair, and secreting neuroprotective factors. Though not naturally dopaminergic, they show promise in Parkinson’s therapy through immunomodulation and support of endogenous repair, with various delivery methods under investigation.
